INTRODUCTION

For our Learning in Museums project, we have created an activity booklet called H2OME, which is designed and written about the topic of Water.

Our hope is that the booklet provides a learning opportunity for museum visitors and their friends/family about the regionâ€™s water story, as well as their own personal home water story.

The booklet is designed for children in grades 3-5, but we highly encourage parents to participate in the activities as well.

In collaboration with Daniel Noh and Don Lee

Discover Interview Insights

Christine Mondor

The principal architect at evolveEA and educator at both Carnegie Mellon University and Chatham University, all while currently serving as Chair of the Pittsburgh Planning Commission.

  • PWSA is a great resource for communication from the utilities companies to the people.
  • Flush It! Is an interesting tool that allows people to see how long a flush from your home toilet in Pittsburgh takes to get to a treatment center. It could be interesting to think about how this kind of data can be incorporated to make the exhibit more interesting.
  • One of the ways we can turn this exhibit into is just a very accessible, enjoyable platform where people can read more complex and complex data.

Mfon Ibekwe and Vasi Vijayashanthar

Mfon and Vasi are 1st year Phd students in the Civil and Environmental Engineering Department. They are both advised by Dr. Jeanne VanBriesen, who is the Director of the Center for Water Quality in Urban Environmental Systems.

  • Pittsburgh has a combined sewer system and is susceptible to combined sewer overflow (CSO)
  • Large amount of data can be found online (such as Pittsburgh H20)
  • Local water utility providers are working hard to mitigate water quality problems
